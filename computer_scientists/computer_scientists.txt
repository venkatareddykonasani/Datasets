Alan Turing:
Pioneering mathematician and computer scientist.
Introduced the Turing Test for AI.

Geoffrey Hinton:
Canadian neural networks and deep learning expert.
Advanced natural language processing (NLP) with backpropagation and convolutional neural networks (CNNs).

Yann LeCun:
French scientist known for his work on CNNs.
Revolutionized AI for image and text processing.

Jurafsky and Martin:
Influential experts in natural language processing.
Authored textbooks and research that have shaped the NLP field.

Ada Lovelace:
Considered the first computer programmer.
Developed algorithms for Charles Babbage's Analytical Engine.

Grace Hopper:
Developed the first compiler for a computer programming language.
Pioneered the development of COBOL, one of the first high-level programming languages.

Andrew Ng:
Co-founder of Google Brain.
Made significant contributions to the development of deep learning and AI education through online courses.

Demis Hassabis:
Co-founder and CEO of DeepMind.
Led the development of AlphaGo, the first computer program to defeat a world champion in the game of Go.

Marvin Minsky:
Co-founder of the MIT Artificial Intelligence Laboratory.
Made foundational contributions to the fields of AI, cognitive psychology, computational linguistics, and robotics.

John McCarthy:
Coined the term "Artificial Intelligence."
Developed Lisp programming language, influential in AI research.

Geoffrey Everest Hinton:
Known as the "Godfather of Deep Learning."
His work on neural networks has been fundamental in the development of deep learning technologies.

Fei-Fei Li:
Led the team that developed ImageNet, a large visual database instrumental for advances in deep learning in image recognition.
Co-founded AI4ALL, a non-profit dedicated to increasing diversity and inclusion in AI education, research, development, and policy.

Yoshua Bengio:
Canadian computer scientist, most noted for his work on artificial neural networks and deep learning.
Along with Geoffrey Hinton and Yann LeCun, considered one of the "three musketeers" of deep learning.


Ray Kurzweil:
An author, computer scientist, inventor, and futurist known for his work on optical character recognition (OCR), text-to-speech synthesis, speech recognition technology, and electronic keyboard instruments.
Advocates for the future role of artificial intelligence in enhancing human capabilities.
